# Episode Engines

## Main Daily Engine

Use this when the episode is about divers.

1. A diver enters the reef.
2. A character misreads what the diver is doing.
3. Turtle suggests watching before reacting.
4. Something funny happens because animals interpret human gear literally.
5. The reef crew turns the event into a tiny rule, joke, or field note.

## Solo Adventure Engine

Use this when one character leads the post.

1. One character goes off alone.
2. They encounter a current, cave, strange animal, or object.
3. Their personality creates the problem.
4. Another character helps them see differently.
5. The adventure returns to the group with a new lesson.

## Neighborhood Problem Engine

Use this for roommate-style filler that still builds the reef.

1. The reef has a small practical problem.
2. Too much sand, a crowded cleaning station, a missing hiding spot, a strange shadow, or a shiny object.
3. The crew solves it badly first.
4. A side character reframes the problem.
5. The reef becomes slightly more lived-in.

## Daily 4-Frame Formula

Frame 1: Set the tiny problem.

Frame 2: Character reacts in their signature way.

Frame 3: Another character complicates or reframes it.

Frame 4: Land the joke, lesson, or wonder.

## Lead Character Loop

- Day 1: Turtle lead
- Day 2: Grumpy Fish lead
- Day 3: Shark lead
- Day 4: Lionfish lead
- Day 5: Group moment
- Repeat

This keeps the feed balanced and prevents one character from carrying the whole brand.

## Example Episodes

### Turtle Lead: First Drift

1. Turtle: "Welcome below."
2. Grumpy Fish: "Keep your fins to yourself."
3. Shark drifts past: "And your dramatic assumptions."
4. Turtle: "The reef is friendlier when you move gently."

### Grumpy Fish Lead: No Coffee, No Talkie

1. Grumpy Fish stares at viewer.
2. Turtle: "Morning."
3. Grumpy Fish: "I have been awake for twelve seconds."
4. Caption: "Some reef residents need a little space."

### Shark Lead: Side-Eye Patrol

1. Shark enters frame looking intense.
2. Grumpy Fish: "Why do you always look like that?"
3. Shark: "This is just my face."
4. Turtle: "Lesson: not every serious face is a threat."

### Lionfish Lead: Look, Don't Touch

1. Lionfish poses dramatically.
2. Shark: "You know you are very extra, right?"
3. Lionfish: "I prefer visually important."
4. Turtle: "Beautiful things still deserve distance."

### Group Lead: Reef Meeting

1. Turtle: "Today's rule: calm movement."
2. Shark: "No panic."
3. Lionfish: "No touching the art."
4. Grumpy Fish: "And no flash photography in my face."

## More 4-Frame Seeds

- A diver appears above the reef. Grumpy Fish calls them bubble machines. Turtle notices this one moves slowly. Shark says, "Suspiciously polite."
- Lionfish poses under blue light. Grumpy Fish asks if she is done. Lionfish says beauty requires patience. Turtle adds, "And distance."
- Shark hides behind coral and fails because he is too large. Grumpy Fish says subtlety has left the reef. Shark says, "I am working with the body I was given."
- Turtle takes the long route around a coral head. Grumpy Fish complains. Turtle points out three tiny creatures everyone would have missed.
- A jellyfish drifts through the reef. Everyone asks where it is going. Jellyfish says the water has already made plans.
- A hermit crab rejects six shells. Shark offers a cave. Hermit Crab says, "That is not a home. That is a weather event."
- A cleaner shrimp hears every argument during cleaning. Grumpy Fish calls it gossip. Cleaner Shrimp calls it maintenance.
- A diver's camera light catches Lionfish. Lionfish enjoys it until it gets too close. Turtle says attention is not the same as respect.
- Grumpy Fish photobombs the calm diver's photo. He pretends it was an accident. The next day he asks whether the angle was flattering.
- Shark sees a boat shadow. Everyone expects him to be brave. He admits loud shadows feel different from below.

## Filler Ideas That Still Build the World

- Grumpy Fish starts a no-bubbles-before-breakfast policy.
- Turtle takes the longest possible route to prove rushing ruins everything.
- Shark practices smiling and makes everyone more nervous.
- Lionfish gives a lecture called "Admire From Over There."
- Grumpy Fish thinks a dive camera is a giant eye.
- Turtle mistakes a diver's hand signal for a dance invitation.
- Shark tries hiding behind coral and is obviously too large.
- Lionfish refuses to move because the lighting is finally correct.
- Grumpy Fish opens a complaint desk in a sponge.
- Turtle starts naming currents like old friends.
- Shark quietly escorts a nervous small fish through open water.
- Lionfish hosts a do-not-touch seminar nobody asked for.
- A jellyfish drifts through and accidentally gives perfect life advice.
- A hermit crab keeps moving into worse and worse homes.
- A cleaner shrimp becomes the reef gossip.
- An eel gives mysterious warnings that are technically useless.
- A school of fish acts like a crowd that overreacts to everything.
- A stingray appears from the sand and scares everyone by existing.
- A pufferfish gets offended when everyone waits for it to puff.
- A tiny goby claims to own the whole reef.
- A crab finds a bottle cap and declares itself royalty.
