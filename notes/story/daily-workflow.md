# Daily Story Workflow

## Purpose

Use this workflow to make consistent daily ScubaWithMe content from the story world without needing to invent the whole universe every morning.

The goal is attention first, but not empty attention. Each post should leave the viewer with one of these:

- a small laugh
- a tiny ocean truth
- a character moment
- a feeling of calm discovery
- a reason to come back tomorrow

## Daily Steps

1. Pick the lead character.
2. Pick the story engine: diver encounter, solo adventure, or reef neighborhood problem.
3. Pick one tiny emotion or observation.
4. Write the 4 frames.
5. Make sure the final frame lands on a joke, lesson, or wonder.
6. Match the post to one current selected image or character asset.
7. Keep the caption short and in the ScubaWithMe voice.
8. Save any strong episode ideas back into `notes/story/episode-engines.md` or a future episode bank.

## Rotation

- Monday: Turtle
- Tuesday: Grumpy Fish
- Wednesday: Shark
- Thursday: Lionfish
- Friday: Group reef moment
- Saturday: Side character, adventure, or current episode
- Sunday: quiet field-note style post

## Caption Formula

Moment: what the viewer is seeing.

Feeling: why it mattered.

Detail: species, location, behavior, or camera note if available.

Invitation: soft prompt to follow, comment, or view the collection.

## Example Caption

The reef gets quieter when you stop trying to arrive somewhere.

Turtle noticed the slow route first. Grumpy Fish complained the entire way. Shark pretended not to listen.

Some dives are less about finding the biggest thing and more about noticing what was already beside you.

## Quality Rules

- Do not make every post a sales post.
- Do not claim print readiness because an image looks good online.
- Do not make false species, location, behavior, or conservation claims.
- Keep humor gentle, dry, and character-led.
- Avoid childish punchlines unless intentionally testing a kid-friendly side line.
- Do not show touching, chasing, feeding, grabbing, or riding marine animals.
- Keep the brand photography-led. The cartoon story should support the images, not replace them.

## Practical Formats

### Instagram Carousel

- 4 slides, one frame per slide.
- Strongest format for the 4-frame story.
- Final slide should be shareable as a tiny quote, joke, or rule.

### Instagram Reel

- 4 panels shown one at a time.
- Add simple movement: bubbles, slow drift, slight zoom, type appearing.
- Keep it readable without sound.

### Story Sequence

- One frame per story.
- Use polls sparingly: "Trust the bubble stranger?" or "Would Grumpy Fish approve?"

### Product Seeds

If a post performs well, consider whether it can become:

- sticker
- postcard
- notebook cover
- small print
- phone case graphic
- social avatar set

Do not turn every episode into a product. Let the audience tell us which moments have pull.
