# Below With Me: Series Bible

## Premise

`Below With Me` is a daily 4-frame underwater comic world for ScubaWithMe.

A small reef community lives near a popular dive path. They have heard stories about divers: noisy bubbles, bright lights, awkward fins, and cameras that get too close. Most of the reef avoids them.

One day, a calm turtle notices that one diver keeps returning, moving slowly, watching respectfully, and never touching anything. The reef crew starts arguing about whether this surface creature is dangerous, annoying, interesting, or maybe even part of the reef's story.

The series follows Turtle, Grumpy Fish, Shark, and Lionfish as they slowly learn how to understand divers, protect their reef, and invite viewers into underwater life without turning the animals into pets or props.

## Main Emotional Arc

- The reef starts suspicious of divers.
- Turtle is curious but cautious.
- Grumpy Fish thinks all divers are rude bubble machines.
- Shark is tired of being feared and photographed like a monster.
- Lionfish likes attention but insists on distance.
- The animals slowly learn the difference between careless visitors and respectful observers.
- The diver becomes a recurring silent presence, almost like the audience.
- The reef crew learns to interact by setting boundaries: look, drift, notice, do not chase, do not touch.
- The final message is not "humans belong everywhere."
- The final message is: "If you enter gently, the ocean lets you witness."

## Coherent Long-Term Story

The story moves from:

1. `Divers are scary.`
2. `Some visitors rush and disturb. Some visitors slow down and witness.`
3. `The reef does not perform for humans, but it can be seen by those who respect it.`

This supports ScubaWithMe because underwater photography becomes more than decoration. It becomes a relationship with a place, a subject, and a moment.

## The Diver

The diver should mostly remain silent and partly offscreen. Show them through:

- bubbles
- camera light
- slow movement
- hand signals
- fins
- shadows
- a respectful pause
- backing away when an animal needs space

The diver works best as a viewer stand-in. They are not the hero of the reef. They are a guest learning how to see.

## Story Rules

- The animals do not become pets.
- The diver does not touch, feed, chase, or casually rescue animals.
- Humor comes from misunderstanding, personality, reef life, and human gear being interpreted oddly.
- Every few episodes should include one tiny real ocean truth.
- Keep the human mostly offscreen so the viewer feels like the diver.
- The reef crew can become curious about divers, but they stay wild.
- Interaction means respectful observation, not contact.
- The reef is not a stage, even when Lionfish says it is.
- Use wonder as the final flavor more often than sales.

## Brand Fit

The story should feel:

- curious
- warm
- respectful of marine life
- visually observant
- calm but not sleepy
- funny in small doses
- lightly educational without sounding preachy

Avoid:

- loud mascot comedy
- fake conservation claims
- touristy scuba cliches
- treating dangerous or delicate animals as toys
- making the diver the savior
- turning the comic into generic ocean decor
