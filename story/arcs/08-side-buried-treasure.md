# Arc: Buried Treasure

## Premise

Grumpy Fish finds something shiny in the sand. The reef argues over whether it is treasure, trash, history, or trouble.

## Main Uses

- comedy
- human-object mystery
- cleanup theme
- reef neighborhood conflict

## Plot Points

- Grumpy Fish sees something shiny.
- Everyone thinks it is treasure.
- Shark thinks it smells wrong.
- Lionfish wants it as a stage prop.
- Turtle says old objects tell stories, but not all stories are good.
- Crab declares the object royal property.
- Cleaner Shrimp claims to know who dropped it.
- The quiet diver later notices it.
- The diver removes it gently or marks it without disturbing the reef.
- The crew debates whether humans are careless or curious.

## Possible Ending

The shiny thing becomes a lesson: care includes cleanup, but the reef does not exist to make humans feel heroic.

## 8-Episode Outline

### Episode 1: The Spark In The Sand

Lead: Grumpy Fish

4-frame outline:

1. Grumpy Fish sees a glint under the sand.
2. He calls it suspicious.
3. Lionfish calls it treasure.
4. Shark says, "Those can be the same thing."

Final turn: mystery hook.

### Episode 2: Treasure Committee

Lead: Group

4-frame outline:

1. Turtle suggests observing before touching.
2. Grumpy Fish says committees ruin treasure.
3. Lionfish says the object needs a curator.
4. Shark says, "It needs not to explode."

Final turn: group personality clash.

### Episode 3: Crab Crown

Lead: Crab

4-frame outline:

1. Crab climbs onto the shiny object.
2. Crab declares himself ruler of the sparkle.
3. Grumpy Fish asks for legal proof.
4. Crab says, "I am standing on it."

Final turn: side character comedy.

### Episode 4: Cleaner Shrimp Knows A Guy

Lead: Cleaner Shrimp

4-frame outline:

1. Cleaner Shrimp claims the object came from above.
2. Grumpy Fish asks how she knows.
3. Cleaner Shrimp says everyone talks during cleaning.
4. Turtle says, "The reef has records. They are just tiny."

Final turn: gossip as world-building.

### Episode 5: Not Reef-Made

Lead: Turtle

4-frame outline:

1. More sand moves away from the object.
2. Turtle sees it has hard human edges.
3. Lionfish says it ruins the composition.
4. Turtle says, "It did not grow here."

Final turn: environmental realization.

### Episode 6: The Quiet Bubble Acts

Lead: Turtle

4-frame outline:

1. The quiet diver notices the object.
2. The reef crew watches from cover.
3. The diver removes or marks it carefully.
4. Grumpy Fish says, "I am not clapping. I am blinking aggressively."

Final turn: cautious appreciation.

### Episode 7: What Humans Leave

Lead: Shark

4-frame outline:

1. The crew looks at the cleared sand.
2. Shark says humans leave fear and objects.
3. Turtle says some also leave space.
4. Grumpy Fish says, "They could leave snacks. But no feeding. I know."

Final turn: nuance with joke.

### Episode 8: Almost Normal

Lead: Turtle

4-frame outline:

1. Small fish return to the patch.
2. Sand softens the edges of the disturbance.
3. Lionfish says the scene has recovered aesthetically.
4. Turtle says, "Almost normal is still worth remembering."

Final turn: gentle responsibility.
