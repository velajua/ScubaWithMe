# Master Plot Points

This is the broad plot-point bank. Do not treat it as chapters yet. Each bullet can later become a single 4-frame post, a mini arc, a carousel, a reel, a product quote, or a larger season beat.

## First Contact With Divers

- A diver appears at the reef for the first time.
- Grumpy Fish declares an emergency.
- Turtle says, "Wait. Watch how they move."
- Shark hides because divers always point cameras at him.
- Lionfish poses, then gets offended when nobody understands personal space.
- The diver backs away from Lionfish instead of chasing.
- Turtle notices that the diver did not push closer.
- Grumpy Fish refuses to trust anything with that many bubbles.
- The reef mistakes bubbles for a warning signal.
- Turtle wonders whether bubbles can mean breathing instead of danger.
- Shark says humans only like scary stories about him.
- A diver's camera light makes Lionfish think she is onstage.
- A careless diver from another group kicks sand everywhere.
- The reef crew blames all divers.
- The quiet diver returns and hovers away from the coral.
- Turtle starts calling them "the quiet bubble."
- Grumpy Fish calls the diver "the bubble machine" even after learning better.
- The crew watches from hiding to see whether the quiet bubble repeats the same respectful behavior.
- Shark realizes being seen does not always mean being hunted.
- Grumpy Fish photobombs a diver photo by accident.
- Grumpy Fish later pretends the photobomb was strategic.
- Lionfish explains that beauty is not an invitation to touch.
- Turtle turns that into the reef's first rule.

## Learning To Interact

- The animals learn that interaction does not mean touching.
- Turtle defines the first reef rule: look, drift, notice.
- Grumpy Fish adds: no bubbles before breakfast.
- Shark adds: no dramatic assumptions.
- Lionfish adds: admire from a distance.
- The diver backs away when an animal turns away.
- The reef crew realizes moving away can be a clear answer.
- A new fish panics when a diver arrives.
- Turtle explains how to watch the diver's movement instead of only the shape.
- Shark explains that a slow path is different from a chase.
- Lionfish demonstrates a boundary by turning her fins outward.
- Grumpy Fish misunderstands and thinks Lionfish is doing theater.
- The quiet diver recognizes the same reef animals over several visits.
- The animals recognize the diver's fins, hand signals, camera, and slow approach.
- The reef crew learns to stay calm without becoming careless.
- The reef becomes confident enough to keep living while the quiet diver watches.
- The final lesson is not trust everyone. It is notice behavior.

## Reef Rules

- Look, do not touch.
- No chasing.
- No sandstorms.
- No flash in sleepy faces.
- Distance is respect.
- The reef is not a souvenir.
- If the animal moves away, the answer is no.
- Beauty is not an invitation.
- Serious faces are not threats by default.
- Bubbles are not conversation.
- Do not crowd a cleaning station.
- The reef was busy before you arrived.
- A good visitor leaves the scene still feeling wild.

## Roommate And Neighborhood Fillers

- Grumpy Fish starts a no-bubbles-before-breakfast policy.
- Turtle takes the longest possible route to prove rushing ruins everything.
- Shark practices smiling and makes everyone more nervous.
- Lionfish gives a lecture called "Admire From Over There."
- Grumpy Fish thinks a dive camera is a giant eye.
- Turtle mistakes a diver's hand signal for a dance invitation.
- Shark tries hiding behind coral and is obviously too large.
- Lionfish refuses to move because the lighting is finally correct.
- Grumpy Fish opens a complaint desk in a sponge.
- Turtle starts naming currents like old friends.
- Shark quietly escorts a nervous small fish through open water.
- Lionfish hosts a do-not-touch seminar nobody asked for.
- A jellyfish drifts through and accidentally gives perfect life advice.
- A hermit crab keeps moving into worse and worse homes.
- A cleaner shrimp becomes the reef gossip.
- An eel gives mysterious warnings that are technically useless.
- A school of fish acts like a crowd that overreacts to everything.
- A stingray appears from the sand and scares everyone by existing.
- A pufferfish gets offended when everyone waits for it to puff.
- A tiny goby claims to own the whole reef.
- A crab finds a bottle cap and declares itself royalty.
- Shark gives excellent advice while accidentally looming in silhouette.
- Lionfish refers to a small rock as "the stage."
- Grumpy Fish pretends not to care and then attends every reef meeting.
- Turtle pauses long enough that everyone thinks he forgot the question.
- The reef meeting always goes off topic.

## Current Adventure

- A current picks up unexpectedly.
- Turtle rides it farther than planned.
- At first it feels peaceful.
- Familiar coral disappears.
- Turtle meets Jellyfish, who is not worried about drifting.
- Jellyfish says, "The water has already made plans."
- Turtle learns that being calm does not mean being passive.
- Grumpy Fish pretends not to be worried.
- Shark reads the current better than anyone expected.
- Lionfish insists search parties need better lighting.
- The crew follows clues: sand trails, bubbles, shell markers, and current direction.
- Turtle returns with a new route name: the blue hallway, sandy quiet, or shadow garden.
- The crew learns that the reef is bigger than their neighborhood.

## Buried Treasure

- Grumpy Fish sees something shiny in the sand.
- Everyone thinks it is treasure.
- Shark thinks it smells wrong.
- Lionfish thinks it belongs in her performance area.
- Turtle says old objects tell stories, but not all stories are good.
- The shiny thing might be a dropped dive light, bottle cap, old coin, camera part, shell, or piece of human trash.
- The crew argues over ownership.
- Crab declares the object royal property.
- Cleaner Shrimp claims to know who dropped it.
- The object partly disappears under sand again.
- The quiet diver later notices it.
- The diver removes it gently or marks it without disturbing the reef.
- The crew debates whether humans are careless or curious.
- The lesson becomes: care includes cleanup, but the reef does not exist to make humans feel heroic.

## Moving Houses

- A storm rearranges sand and loose coral.
- Grumpy Fish's favorite hiding spot fills in.
- Hermit Crab needs a new shell.
- Turtle tries to organize a calm search.
- Shark keeps suggesting huge caves no one else can use.
- Lionfish judges every option by lighting.
- Grumpy Fish rejects every spot for being too open, too sandy, too popular, or too cheerful.
- Hermit Crab tries a shell with "character" and hates it.
- Tiny Goby claims every rock is already occupied.
- The crew finds several possible homes.
- Someone realizes the best home is not the prettiest one.
- The lesson becomes: home is shelter, memory, and neighbors.

## Night Dive

- The reef changes after dark.
- Familiar coral becomes strange.
- Jellyfish and glowing plankton appear.
- Shark becomes calmer at night.
- Grumpy Fish hates everything after sunset.
- Lionfish becomes extra dramatic under dive lights.
- Turtle teaches that the ocean has many moods.
- The calm diver returns for a night dive.
- The diver's light reveals details without chasing them.
- The crew discovers that the same reef can become a whole new world.

## One-Off Character Encounters

- Jellyfish drifts in with dreamy advice.
- Hermit Crab treats shells like real estate.
- Cleaner Shrimp knows everyone's secrets.
- Moray Eel looks scary but wants privacy.
- Stingray appears from the sand and says it was there the whole time.
- Octopus leaves shell messages and disappears.
- Pufferfish refuses to be treated like a party trick.
- Tiny Goby acts like a reef king.
- Crab finds human trash and turns it into a crown.

## Character-Specific Leads

- Turtle leads slow observation episodes.
- Grumpy Fish leads complaint, suspicion, and accidental-softness episodes.
- Shark leads misunderstood, fear, shadow, and calm-strength episodes.
- Lionfish leads beauty, boundaries, distance, and attention episodes.
- Group episodes turn the reef into a neighborhood.
- Side character episodes expand the world without breaking the main cast loop.
