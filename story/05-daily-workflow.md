# Daily Story Workflow

## Goal

Create daily attention-friendly ScubaWithMe content without losing the brand's core values: underwater wonder, calm discovery, and respectful marine observation.

## Daily Steps

1. Pick the lead character from the loop.
2. Pick the story engine: diver encounter, solo adventure, or neighborhood problem.
3. Pick one tiny emotion or observation.
4. Write four frames.
5. Make frame four land on a joke, lesson, or wonder.
6. Match the script to a current selected image or future character asset.
7. Keep the caption short.
8. Save any strong new idea back into the story workspace.

## Post Types

### Instagram Carousel

- Four slides.
- One frame per slide.
- Best format for the core comic.

### Reel

- Four panels shown one at a time.
- Use slow drift, bubbles, zoom, or simple type reveals.
- Keep it readable without sound.

### Story Sequence

- One frame per story.
- Optional low-pressure poll: "Trust the quiet bubble?" or "Would Grumpy Fish approve?"

### Product Seeds

Only consider products after a post proves it has pull.

Possible products:

- sticker
- postcard
- notebook cover
- phone case graphic
- small print
- character quote card

## Caption Formula

Moment: what the viewer is seeing.

Feeling: why it mattered.

Detail: species, location, behavior, or camera note if available.

Invitation: soft prompt to follow, comment, or view the collection.

## Example Caption

The reef gets quieter when you stop trying to arrive somewhere.

Turtle noticed the slow route first. Grumpy Fish complained the entire way. Shark pretended not to listen.

Some dives are less about finding the biggest thing and more about noticing what was already beside you.
