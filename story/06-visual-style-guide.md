# Visual Style Guide

## Simple Drawn Comic Style

Use this style for the `Below With Me` 4-frame story images.

The goal is not polished concept art. The goal is a charming simple drawing that feels like an upcoming artist made it by hand: clear, warm, imperfect, and easy to repeat.

## Core Style

- Simple hand-drawn comic.
- Loose pencil-and-ink linework.
- Flat watercolor or marker-style color.
- Light sketchbook texture.
- Slight imperfections are good.
- Characters should be recognizable, but not anatomically detailed.
- Keep the panels readable at phone size.

## Must Feel Like

- an early indie comic
- a simple sketchbook panel
- an artist still developing a personal style
- warm and approachable
- handmade, not corporate

## Avoid

- photorealism
- realistic painting
- polished digital concept art
- 3D renders
- vector clip art
- anime style
- overly cute mascot branding
- extreme cartoon proportions
- neon colors
- horror mood
- busy reef detail

## Character Treatment

### Grumpy Fish

- Small, simple reef fish.
- Big annoyed or alarmed eyes.
- Slight frown or skeptical mouth.
- Funny, not ugly.

### Turtle

- Calm and gentle.
- Rounded but still recognizable as a sea turtle.
- Wise, relaxed expression.
- Not a logo turtle and not a mascot turtle.

### Shark

- Calm reef shark.
- Mildly offended or deadpan expression.
- Not aggressive, bloody, scary, or monstrous.
- The joke is that everyone assumes the shark is the problem.

### Reef

- Simple coral silhouettes and shapes.
- Enough background to feel underwater.
- Do not crowd the panel.
- Use a few bubbles, shadows, and soft blue water texture.

## Color

Use:

- light underwater blue
- seafoam
- muted coral
- sandy beige
- gentle reef green
- soft blue shadows

Avoid:

- high-saturation neon reef colors
- heavy black shadows
- overprocessed cyan
- one-note blue-only scenes

## Panel Rules

- Use a clean 2x2 grid for normal episodes.
- Use thick hand-drawn panel borders.
- Keep each frame readable with one main action.
- Use simple speech bubbles with short text.
- Make sure text is large, dark, and legible.
- Do not overload a panel with background detail.

## Prompt Checklist

Before generating, include:

- `simple hand-drawn comic by an upcoming artist`
- `loose pencil-and-ink lines`
- `flat watercolor or marker colors`
- `minimal detail`
- `not realistic`
- `not polished concept art`
- `clear 2x2 comic panels`
- exact speech bubble text
- no logos or watermark

## Existing Image Style Check

Before generating a new story image, check the current generated images already saved inside `story/arcs/`.

Use the existing arc images as the local style reference so the series stays visually consistent across episodes.

Current reference image:

- `story/arcs/01-season-bubble-stranger/01-episode-the-bubble-alarm.png`

When matching the style, look for:

- simple upcoming-artist hand-drawn feel
- loose linework
- flat marker or watercolor color
- simplified animal shapes
- thick hand-drawn panel borders
- readable but imperfect comic lettering
- uncluttered reef backgrounds
- warm, funny underwater tone

Do not drift toward:

- more realistic anatomy
- polished concept art
- highly rendered lighting
- 3D or vector-clean edges
- busier coral backgrounds
- more complex character designs

If future episodes introduce a better approved style reference, add it to this list instead of replacing or deleting earlier references.
